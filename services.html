Mine and Geological Service

Mineral resources and the environment, as well as playing an important role in regulating the mining industry in order to achieve a balance between resource utilization and environmental protection.

Licensing and Regulation

Providing the necessary permits and regulating mining activities. This is crucial to ensure that mining operations comply with environmental standards, safety regulations, and applicable laws.

Mineral Exploration

Offering exploration services to identify and assess the potential of mineral deposits. This is the initial stage in the development of mining projects and allows companies to determine the existing resource potential.

Environmental Monitoring

Conducting environmental impact assessments and monitoring the environmental effects of mining operations, including water and air quality monitoring and waste management. This is essential for maintaining environmental sustainability and compliance.

Geological Data Provision

Providing geological data and information that can be used by mining companies, researchers, and the general public. This information is crucial for a better understanding of the geological potential of a region.

Laboratory Analysis

This analysis is conducted to determine various properties and characteristics of the coal, which are important for multiple purposes, including energy production, environmental assessment, and quality control.

Proximate Analysis

This includes measuring the moisture content, volatile matter, fixed carbon, and ash content of coal. It is essential because this information is used to evaluate coal's combustion capability and energy potential.

Ultimate Analysis

This analysis determines the major chemical element composition of coal, including carbon, hydrogen, nitrogen, sulfur, and oxygen content. This data is crucial for understanding the coal's chemical properties and environmental impact.

Calorific Value

The measurement of coal's calorific value indicates the amount of heat energy released during combustion. It is highly relevant for energy applications, such as power generation.

Sulfur Content

Analyzing the sulfur content in coal is important because sulfur emissions can lead to the release of toxic gases during combustion. Reducing sulfur content is often a goal in coal processing to mitigate environmental impacts.
