MEDIUM COAL
GAR 4200 - GAR 4600

Medium-grade coal with a Gross as Received (GAR) calorific value ranging from 4200 to 4600 kilocalories per kilogram (kcal/kg). This is coal with moderate energy content and is commonly used in various industrial and energy applications. While not considered high-energy coal, coal in this category can still generate significant heat for many purposes.

MEDIUM HIGH
GAR 5200 - GAR 5500

Medium-grade coal with a Gross as Received (GAR) calorific value ranging from 5200 to 5500 kilocalories per kilogram (kcal/kg). This type of coal has moderate to high energy content and is frequently used in industrial applications and power generation where substantial heat output is required. Coal in this category has higher energy content compared to Medium-grade coal.

HIGH CALORIE COAL
GAR 6200 - GAR 5800

High-energy coal with a Gross as Received (GAR) calorific value ranging from 6200 to 5800 kilocalories per kilogram (kcal/kg). This coal type has high energy content and is typically used in various industrial applications and power generation where a significant heat output is crucial. It has the highest energy content among the mentioned types.

LOW RANK COAL

Low-rank coal refers to a category of coal characterized by lower carbon content and lower energy density compared to higher-rank coals. This type of coal is typically found in the early stages of coal formation and is considered less mature than higher-rank coals. Low-rank coal often contains high moisture content, lower calorific value, and higher reactivity. Despite its lower energy content, low-rank coal still has various applications, especially in locations where higher-rank coals are not economically available.
